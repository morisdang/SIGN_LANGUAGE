Our Project

Problem

Voice-enabled assistants offer a range of useful features, including automated speech recognition (ASR) and machine translation. However, these technologies are often inaccessible to the over 70 million Deaf people worldwide who use sign language, and the 1.5+ billion people globally affected by hearing loss.

Fingerspelling, a component of sign language that uses hand shapes to represent letters, is often faster than typing on a smartphone. Despite this, sign language recognition AI for text entry is behind voice-to-text or gesture-based typing due to the lack of robust datasets.

Solve

We propose a novel deep learning model to solve the problem of sign language recognition. Our model uses mediapipe and transformer to extract and encode the hand gestures and facial expressions of the signers. We then use a decoder to generate the corresponding spoken language text. We evaluate our model on several sign language datasets and show that it achieves state-of-the-art performance. Our model is written in English and can be easily adapted to other languages.
